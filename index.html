Optimizing Padi MR 297 Growth Dynamics

Malaysia's self-sufficiency level (SSL) for rice currently hovers around 65% to 70%. To safeguard national food security, agronomists utilize mathematical models to track crop health. **Padi MR 297**, developed by MARDI, has a maturation cycle of 110–115 days.

By monitoring cumulative growth $f(x)$ (plant height) and evaluating its **first derivative $f'(x)$ (instantaneous daily growth rate)**, we pinpoint the exact moments of maximum metabolic activity. This tells farmers precisely when to apply nitrogen fertilizers to prevent waste and maximize grain yields.

The Mathematical Model (Logistic Growth)

Cumulative Growth Function: $f(x)$
$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$

Where $L$ is mature plant height (cm), $k$ is growth coefficient, and $x_0$ is inflection day (point of fastest growth).

First Derivative (Growth Rate): $f'(x) = \frac{df}{dx}$
$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x-x_0)}}{\left(1 + e^{-k(x-x_0)}\right)^2}$$

Measures the exact rate of vertical growth (cm/day) on Day $x$ (Days After Transplanting - DAT).
